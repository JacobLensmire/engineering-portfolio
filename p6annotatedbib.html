Annotated Bibliography (Final Project)

Liu, Q.-Q., Xu, X.-P., Yang, X.-J., Xiong, J., & Hu, Y.-T. (2022). Distinguishing Different Types of Mobile Phone Addiction: Development and Validation of the Mobile Phone Addiction Type Scale (MPATS) in Adolescents and Young Adults. International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health, 19(5), Article 5. https://doi.org/10.3390/ijerph19052593
This source outlines research into different types of mobile phone addiction, and the development of a Mobile Phone Addiction Type Scale (MPATS) for adolescents and young adults. The study concludes that the MPATS has good construct validity, and is a suitable tool in measuring multiple types of mobile phone addiction in adolescents. This article allows for further specification when looking at mobile phone addiction, and when developing a solution, will aid in discovering which specific type of mobile phone addiction is being seen. The article does not further analyze the results of their tests, but provides the symptoms that will allow for the user to further understand mobile phone addiction.
Naik, R., Sk, K., & Js, S. (2019). Mobile Phone Addiction: Symptoms, Impacts and Causes-A Review.

Choliz, M. (2012). Mobile-phone addiction in adolescence: The Test of Mobile Phone Dependence (TMD). Prog Health Sci, 2, 33–44.
TThis source presents an instrument, the Test of Mobile Phone Dependence (TMD), constructed to determine dependence on mobile devices in adolescents. It outlines the development of this instrument, the results of administering it, and an analysis of those results. It finds that this instrument will likely be useful in researching and treating mobile phone addiction. This relates directly to our topic because understanding the symptoms and characterizations of mobile phone addiction is an important step in creating a solution. The source clearly recognizes what it accomplishes, and addresses its weaknesses in not further analyzing the results, which is important to not cause assumptions and interpretations that the study does not actually address. This source provides important base knowledge in relation to the other sources, as it creates a framework with which to view the effects of mobile phone addiction and ability to treat it.
